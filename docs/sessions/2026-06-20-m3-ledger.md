## 5. Naming decisions

**Approved/recommended names** (adjustable at draft review):
- Result types: `RankerResult`, `RankedOutfit`, `ScoreBreakdown`, `RankerContext`.
- Enum: `FallbackStage{none, overuse_relaxed, variant_cap_relaxed, cooldown_relaxed, insufficient}`.
- Inputs: `item_affinity`, `liked_full_signatures`, `shown_full_signatures`, `recent_disliked_base_keys`,
  `recent_disliked_item_ids`, `contextual_disliked_item_ids`, `locked_item_ids`.
- **N3 lock-starvation diagnostic (explicitly approved ‚Äî descriptive pair):**
  `locked_survivor_count: int` (always = candidates surviving the lock filter) +
  `insufficient_locked_candidates: bool` (true iff locks were requested **and** count < k). State-oriented;
  M3 reports, M5 decides re-entry. Parallels `insufficient_wardrobe`.

## 7. M3 boundary (out of scope ‚Äî do not let creep in)

No GPT/parse/JSON-repair (M2/M5); no M2 validation relaxation (validation never relaxes, even under
fallback); no candidate creation (M3 only drops/reorders, never adds); no rescue/Spearhead path/risk metric;
no learned-scorer/graph behavior (M6); no DB/cache/`generationIndex` lifecycle/`GenerationSnapshot` (M4/M5);

deletion (deletion license activates M5/M6). Shown-history and affinity **storage** stay out (H19/M4) ‚Äî M3
takes them as pre-reduced inputs.

## 8. Test obligations (beyond happy-path)

Dominance tests per ¬ß5 (`:39-40`): each scoring term has a breakdown entry **and** a test it cannot dominate
when it should not ‚Äî with the **known exception** that a 4-item itemBoost (~+8) can exceed comboBoost (+2)
(¬ß11`:424`), pinned as a visible eval-tracked case, not hidden. Plus the mutants likely to survive a careless
suite: `>=15` for the gate ¬∑ `source_index` as final tiebreak (kills re-roll) ¬∑ recomputing overuse after
relax ¬∑ re-deduping FullSignature in M3 ¬∑ relaxing locks/dislikes in fallback ¬∑ variant cap keeping bottom-2 ¬∑
sign-flip on `COOLDOWN_PENALTY` ¬∑ accumulating (not flat) dislikePenalty ¬∑ diversity tie-break as a static
sort instead of "least-represented so far."
