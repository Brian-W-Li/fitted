# Problem Scenario

## Context

Alex is a busy college student who owns a variety of clothes but often feels like they have “nothing to wear.” Their wardrobe is spread across drawers and hangers, and they usually fall back on the same familiar outfits. When an event or situation comes up (such as attending class, giving a presentation, going to a social gathering, or going on a date) Alex spends an unnecessary amount of time deciding what to wear.

## Problem

Choosing an outfit feels stressful and uncertain. Alex worries about whether their clothes match the occasion, the weather, or their desired impression. Existing solutions are either generic fashion advice that doesn’t account for what Alex already owns, or require significant effort, such as manually planning outfits or searching for inspiration online.

As a result, Alex:
- Wastes time deciding what to wear
- Lacks confidence in outfit choices
- Underutilizes much of their wardrobe

## Goal

Alex wants a simple way to get outfit suggestions that:
- Are personalized to their own wardrobe
- Fit the specific situation or occasion
- Reduce decision fatigue and stress

The ultimate goal is to feel confident, prepared, and satisfied with what they are wearing without having to buy new clothes or overthink the process.
