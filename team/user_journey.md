# User Journey

## User Goal / Need / Desire

The user’s primary goal is to quickly choose an outfit they feel confident wearing for a specific situation.  
They want personalized guidance that uses their existing wardrobe and reduces the time and mental effort required to decide what to wear.

---

## Motivation and Entry

- The user encounters a situation where they need to decide what to wear (e.g., class, interview, social event).
- They feel uncertain, rushed, or overwhelmed by choices.
- They seek out a solution that can help them make a confident decision without excessive effort.

---

## Interaction and Exploration

- The user engages with the product by representing what clothing they already own.
- The user communicates their current need or context (such as the type of occasion or desired style).
- The system provides outfit suggestions that align with the user’s wardrobe and expressed needs.
- The user reviews and compares options, refining their choices as needed.

---

## Outcome and Value Exchange

- The user selects an outfit that feels appropriate and confidence-boosting.
- The original need, choosing what to wear efficiently and confidently, is fulfilled.
- The user saves time, reduces stress, and gains better insight into how to use their wardrobe.

---

## High-Level User Stories

- As a user, I want help deciding what to wear so I don’t feel stressed or uncertain.
- As a user, I want outfit suggestions based on my own clothes so they feel relevant.
- As a user, I want confidence that my outfit fits the situation I’m dressing for.
- As a user, I want to feel like I’m making better use of my wardrobe.

---

## Summary

This journey captures how a user moves from uncertainty and decision fatigue to confidence and satisfaction.  
The value exchange is clear: the user gains clarity, confidence, and saved time, while the product becomes a trusted tool for repeated outfit decisions.
